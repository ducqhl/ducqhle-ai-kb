# Science as the New Turing Test

## ELI5
The classic [[The Turing Test|Turing Test]] asks: can an AI fool a human into thinking it's human? Percy thinks that's the wrong bar now — we *want* AI to be better than humans, not just human-like, and static tests are easy to game. His proposed replacement: count how many genuinely new scientific discoveries the AI produces. If it cures cancer or cracks fusion, nobody can call that cheating — even if it somehow "cheated," you still cured cancer.

## Core Concept
Percy argues the [[The Turing Test]] is no longer a good measure of progress, for two reasons:
1. **Imitation is the wrong goal** — we want AI to be *better* than humans (e.g. far more reliable), not merely indistinguishable.
2. **Humans as the benchmark is shortsighted** — capping the target at human level undersells what AI should do.

**Problem with most tests:** they're **static** — a system "enters a room" and a single score comes out. Static tests are **gameable**.

**His proposal — an online, ungameable metric:** measure **how many new scientific discoveries AI produces**. Curing cancer, inventing a new material, figuring out fusion — these **can't be gamed**, because if you somehow "cheat" your way to curing cancer, you still cured cancer ("good for you").
