# Three Paradigms of AI

## ELI5
AI grew from three rival camps that often thought the others were wrong — one about rules, one about brain-like nets, one about math. Today's AI is a melting pot of all three: the rule camp gave the dream, the net camp gave the engine, the math camp gave the discipline. The fights were real, but the winner used all of them.

## Core Concept
Liang frames AI history as three threads with **fierce debates** but **deep connections**:

| Paradigm | Contribution |
|---|---|
| [[Symbolic AI]] | the **vision** of what an intelligent agent should be |
| [[Neural AI]] | the **model architectures** / how you build models |
| [[Statistical AI]] | the **rigor** (optimization, generalization, train→test language) |

The debates: Minsky & Papert promoted symbolic AI and tried to kill neural nets; statistical ML in the 2000s thought neural nets were dead ("why are you trying to use neural networks?").

The deep connections (the ironies):
- The founding neural-net paper (McCulloch–Pitts) was really about implementing **logical operations** — symbolic in spirit.
- **Go** is a purely *symbolic* game, yet **deep learning** was the key to playing it (AlphaGo).
- Deep learning's first decade was about **perception**, but it has now turned toward **reasoning** — the original goal of symbolic AI.

CS221 deliberately teaches elements of **all three** traditions. AI is a melting pot, and intelligence remains unsolved.
