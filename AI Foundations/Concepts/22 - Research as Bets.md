# Research as Bets

## ELI5
Picking a research topic is like betting on the future — you can't know which idea will pay off, you just try your best. Percy's recipe: look for a big hole in the world that needs filling, pick something where you have a *vision* of where it's going *and* a concrete first step you can actually take today. And aim at problems where you genuinely don't know the answer — because the whole point of research is learning something new, not squeezing out a tiny, forgettable improvement.

## Core Concept
Percy's framework for choosing research:

- **Research = taking bets.** You're betting on the future and can only try your best; nobody automatically knows which topics will pay off.
- **Zoom out and find the big hole.** Look at the world and ask: what gap needs filling *right now*?
- **Vision + concrete first step.** The best research has a clear vision of what you're trying to do **and** a concrete first step you can take immediately. ([[Decentralized Training]] is his example: big vision, with a tractable systems problem to start.)
- **Research taste = work at the boundary.** Pick problems where you **generally don't know the answer** — on the edge of what could and couldn't work. Research is about **information gain**.
- **Avoid low-information wins.** A quick, certain 5% improvement is forgettable in 10 years *unless* it carries a **generalizable lesson**.
