# Societal Impact of AI

## ELI5
One company can build an AI that behaves well for *its* purposes. But what's good for that company might not be good for everybody. And "everybody" don't even agree with each other — different people want different things. So deciding what AI *should* do for the world is less a coding problem and more a people problem, with no single right answer.

## Core Concept
Above the single-developer view ([[Alignment and Developer Goals]]) sits the question: what do *we* want AI to do for **society**? This goes beyond one developer shipping a product, and raises socio-technical concerns:

- privacy
- copyright / intellectual property and generative AI
- impact on jobs and inequality
- geopolitical considerations

Two hard problems make this genuinely difficult:

1. **Who is "we"?** One person? A nation? The whole world? There are **fundamental trade-offs between different people's values** — the same tension seen in politics, independent of AI.
2. **Unintended consequences** — an AI meant to do reasonable things can still produce harmful side effects.

Liang flags these as top concerns "from day one," with a deep dive at the end of the course (which replaced constraint satisfaction problems in the 2025 syllabus).
