# AI Transparency

## ELI5
Five years ago, AI labs published how they built their models. Now they reveal almost nothing. Why? Three reasons: they don't want competitors copying their secrets, they're scared of lawsuits over the data they trained on, and honestly some of it is just nobody having time to write it up. Percy's group built a "transparency index" — basically a scorecard that names which labs share what, hoping that public pressure nudges them to open up.

## Core Concept
Percy on why model transparency has **declined sharply** over the last ~5 years (OpenAI used to publish; now near-silence), and what to do about it.

**Three reasons labs stay opaque:**
1. **Competitive advantage** — training methods are trade secrets; revealing them helps rivals.
2. **Lawsuits** — they train on lots of internet data, some of it "dubious." Laying it out invites litigation.
3. **Mundane: not a priority** — digging up info, writing a report, and getting it through approval takes real engineering effort. The first priority is racing to build the best AI, so nobody has time.

**The fix — a transparency index:** a public scorecard that *calls out* what each lab does and doesn't disclose, raising awareness as a first step toward more openness. Engaging labs has moved some of them over two years — but for **data** specifically there's no incentive, so only **regulation** will move them.

This is also where Percy argues **academia has a unique role**: with "no skin in the game," universities can study copyright/memorization and do fair evaluation that labs (conflict of interest) won't.
