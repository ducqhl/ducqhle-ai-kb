# Symbolic AI

## ELI5
The first big idea for building AI: write down knowledge as rules and symbols, then let the computer reason over them like a logician. "If patient has X and Y, then disease Z." It worked when an expert could spell out every rule — but the real world is messy, and you can't write a rule for everything.

## Core Concept
Symbolic AI is the tradition of representing knowledge as **symbols and logical rules** and reasoning over them. Timeline:

- **1956, Dartmouth workshop** — John McCarthy (later founder of the Stanford AI Lab) convened the leading thinkers to "make a significant advance in two months." They didn't solve much, but **coined the term "artificial intelligence."**
- **1950s optimism** — Logic Theorist (Newell & Simon) proved theorems; automated theorem proving existed this early.
- **1970s–80s, expert systems** — go to a domain expert, have them hand-write the rules (e.g. diagnosing blood infections, converting customer orders). Knowledge closed both the information *and* computation gaps (more knowledge → less blind search). This was **AI's first real industrial impact**.

Shortcomings drove the [[AI Winters]]: rules couldn't handle real-world **uncertainty**, and grew into unmaintainable "spaghetti code."

Symbolic AI's enduring contribution to the field is **the vision** of what an intelligent agent should be.
