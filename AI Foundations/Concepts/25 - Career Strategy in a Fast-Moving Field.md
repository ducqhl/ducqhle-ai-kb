# Career Strategy in a Fast-Moving Field

## ELI5
Your first job isn't a marriage — you'll have many. So don't optimize for prestige or for "keeping up" with peers racking up shiny internships. Optimize for how much you *grow*: work with good people on a mission, in a place where you learn the most. And since AI keeps changing what skills matter, the one skill that never goes stale is the ability to learn fast and adapt.

## Core Concept
Percy's career framework (advice section of the chat), useful as a decision rule:

- **First job ≠ last job.** It's "not marriage" — you'll spend a few years, then move. So prioritize **growth over prestige**.
- **Exploration > exploitation.** Treat post-graduation as continued education; heavily weight the **exploration** part. The rule of thumb: *where would you learn the most?*
- **Good people first.** Working with people you enjoy is the most important factor; setting (academia / startup / team in a big company) is secondary, as long as you're learning what you want.
- **Mission helps.** If you have a real mission (e.g. solve energy), the choice gets much clearer.
- **Ignore the "falling behind" race.** Many top people took **circuitous paths** and switched fields. A sophomore-year internship has little lasting impact; skills + outlook matter, not a CV of "good-sounding names."
- **The one durable skill: learn-to-learn.** In a fast-changing world the only thing that lasts is **how fast you can adapt**. When a model can do your job, can you learn something new? Plus undertrained soft skills: **collaboration**, grit, passion.
